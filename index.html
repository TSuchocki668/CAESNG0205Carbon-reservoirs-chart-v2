Click any bar to learn more about that reservoir.

This chart uses a logarithmic scale on the x-axis. On a regular scale, the deep ocean bar would be about 60 times longer than the terrestrial plants bar, making the smaller reservoirs nearly invisible. The logarithmic scale compresses the larger values so that all six reservoirs are readable at once. Each major gridline represents a tenfold increase in carbon stored.

Chart notes: Sedimentary rock (~100,000,000 GtC) is excluded because it exchanges carbon only on geological timescales of millions of years. Pre-industrial atmospheric carbon was 590 GtC (280 ppm). Current atmospheric carbon is approximately 860 GtC (420 ppm). Sources: Global Carbon Project (2023) and IPCC Sixth Assessment Report (2021).
